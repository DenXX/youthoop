High School Feeder Programs

Seattle & Washington State

A high school feeder program is a youth sports program, operated by high schools, designed to prepare young athletes for participation in high school-level sports. These programs introduce young players to the foundational skills, team dynamics, and training routines that they will encounter in high school sports, ensuring a smoother transition and better preparedness when they reach that level.

Participating in a feeder program can offer numerous benefits such as improved skill development, an early understanding of team strategy, and a higher likelihood of securing a position on competitive high school teams.

Finding Your Local Feeder Program

To find information about feeder programs of your local high school, you can use web search: just type the name of the high school + 'boys|girls select basketball'.

Example search: Redmond Select Boys Basketball

In Washington state, high school select basketball teams play a pivotal role in nurturing the talents of budding athletes and providing them with competitive experiences. These select teams regularly participate in local tournaments where they face off against other school-based select teams.

A prominent example of such competitive platforms is the Eastside Travel League. This league organizes matches that allow teams to test their mettle against each other, fostering camaraderie and elevating the standards of gameplay.

The culmination of their sporting year is the eagerly anticipated state championship tournament held in March. This event not only decides the best high school select team in Washington but also showcases the vibrant basketball culture of the state.
